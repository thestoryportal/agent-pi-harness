Two responses were received (openai:o4-mini and anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6). Our task is to choose the best direction, tally votes, and explain why.

## Decision Criteria

### Risk
- **Without Anti-Patterns:** High potential for irreversible taxonomy sprawl or silent archiving, leading to data loss or user frustration.
- **With Comprehensive Anti-Patterns:** Mitigates AI agent misbehavior, preserves repository quality, reduces support escalations.

### Reward
- **Improved Role File Compliance:** Meets all 11 template checks, streamlines onboarding of AI agents and human partners.
- **Better Governance:** Reduces edge-case errors, improves trust in automated knowledge management.

### Timeline
- **Immediate (1–2 days):** Draft and embed Anti-Patterns section.
- **Short-term (1 week):** Review and integrate other dimension rewrites (handoff tables, iteration protocol).
- **Medium-term (2–4 weeks):** Validate with pilot AI agent and human reviewers.

### Resources
- **Low Effort:** One–two SMEs (HR + Research Director) to draft and approve Anti-Patterns.
- **Existing Templates:** Leverage board’s example rewrites.
- **Minimal Tooling Impact:** No new platforms; just document update.

### New Dimension: AI Governance Impact
- **Governance Concern:** Autonomous AI agents in “AI-Primary” roles require explicit behavioral guardrails.  
- **Impact Assessment:** A dedicated Anti-Patterns section provides those guardrails, lowers risk of unapproved data operations, and aligns with corporate AI ethics policy.

---

## Final Decision

After tallying the two valid board votes, **anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6**’s response clearly offers the deepest, most actionable guidance across all dimensions—even though both responses agree on the missing Anti-Patterns section, Claude’s critique extends to philosophy, handoffs, AI deployment, and Story Portal specificity.

**Next Steps:**
1. **Adopt Claude’s Top Improvement**: Insert a dedicated **Anti-Patterns** section with 3–5 role-specific behavioral failure modes (taxonomy sprawl, silent archiving, completeness theater, etc.).
2. **Integrate Additional Recommendations**: Revise handoff tables and iteration protocol based on Claude’s examples.
3. **Review & Approve** with HR and Research Director within one week.
4. **Publish Updated Role File** and validate with a pilot AI agent operation.

This course of action maximizes risk mitigation, aligns with our AI governance standards, and brings the Research Librarian role file into full compliance with the Story Portal template.
