## Problem Statement
We were asked to evaluate an OKR/Planning Coordinator role template against a Story Portal enterprise AI framework checklist. Board members provided JSON-based ratings across five dimensions and highlighted improvement areas. Our task as CEO is to choose the best direction and explain why.

---

We will adopt Claude’s recommended improvements as our path forward.

## Decision-Making Framework

### Risk
- **Without Anti-Patterns**: AI or humans may drift into “OKR Theater” or autonomy creep (setting goals without buy-in).  
- **Placeholder Sections**: Deploying an AI agent on incomplete context could lead to misaligned facilitation or broken workflows.  

### Reward
- **Immediate Clarity**: Filling gaps and concretizing handoffs and context reduces onboarding friction for AI and human coordinators.  
- **Enhanced Quality**: Role-specific anti-patterns and rewrites improve adoption and guardrails, boosting OKR success rates.  

### Timeline
- **Anti-Patterns Section**: 2 days to draft, review, and integrate.  
- **Context Requirements & Skills**: 1 day to gather artifacts and finalize fields.  
- **Handoff & Story Portal Examples**: 3 days to coordinate with CSO and department heads on formats and priority rules.  

### Resources
- **HR & Strategy Leads**: Lead drafting of Anti-Patterns and finalize context items.  
- **CSO & Dept Heads**: Provide signed artifacts (OKRs, dashboards) for handoff tables.  
- **AI/Dev Team**: Update role template in Story Portal, integrate into AI agent training.  

### AI Readiness (New Dimension)
- Measures whether an AI agent can autonomously begin tasks without manual interventions.
- **Current State**: Placeholders block automation.  
- **Target State**: All context items and operating rules explicitly defined.

## Final Decision
I adopt **anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6**’s recommendations as our blueprint. Our immediate next step is to **add the missing Anti-Patterns section with 3–5 role-specific entries** and fill all placeholder fields in Context Requirements and Required Skills. This highest-priority improvement addresses a direct violation of the template standard and unlocks AI readiness. Once in place, we will refine handoff specificity and Story Portal examples per Claude’s detailed rewrites.

By following this path, we mitigate the greatest risks, deliver rapid quality improvements, and empower both human and AI agents to execute the OKR/Planning Coordinator role with clarity and accountability.
