## Problem Statement

We were tasked with reviewing the **Social Media Manager** role file in our enterprise AI workforce framework, **Story Portal**, and rating it on five dimensions. Each board member delivered a scored JSON with findings and a “top improvement.” As CEO, I must choose which proposal best guides our next steps.

---

## Quick Decision Summary

After evaluating both submissions, we will adopt **anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6**’s proposal as our guiding direction. Claude’s analysis is more granular, highlights critical omissions (especially the missing Anti-Patterns section), and provides precise rewrites. This proposal mitigates high-impact risks and aligns with our hybrid AI-human workflow.

## Decision-Making Framework

To choose between these proposals, I evaluated them along our standard dimensions and added two CEO-level lenses.

### Risk  
- Missing Anti-Patterns exposes us to brand crises, AI misfires, and misinformation, which can damage user trust.  
- Claude pinpoints anti-pattern omission as the single highest-risk item.

### Reward  
- Implementing Claude’s detailed rewrites accelerates operationalization, ensures AI agents and humans follow clear, auditable protocols, and aligns to Story Portal’s festival-community mission.

### Timeline  
- Anti-Patterns section can be drafted in 1 day, reviewed in 1 day, and integrated in our next role-file revision cycle.  
- Philosophy and handoff refinements require 2–3 days of stakeholder workshops.

### Resources  
- We need our HR, Marketing, and AI Governance teams for 2–3 review sessions.  
- No new hires; repurposing existing role-charter and template-maintenance bandwidth.

### New Dimensions

1. **AI-Readiness**  
   - How quickly can an AI agent load the role and act reliably?  
   - Claude’s proposal adds precise STOP criteria and context files, boosting AI readiness.

2. **Operational Specificity**  
   - Do outputs map one-to-one to existing artifacts, formats, and workflows?  
   - Claude provides explicit file names, thresholds, and triggers.

---

## Final Decision

I vote for **anthropic:claude-sonnet-4-6**’s proposal as the foundation for our next iteration. We will:

1. **Add a Role-Specific Anti-Patterns Section**  
   - Define 3–5 hybrid failure modes (AI-drafted crisis responses, rigid scheduling, voice flattening, etc.).  
   - Include STOP criteria and routing protocols per Claude’s examples.

2. **Revise Philosophy Principles**  
   - Implement role-specific, measurable philosophy (e.g., “Story-First Voice”).

3. **Clarify Handoffs**  
   - Map each collaboration to an artifact, format, delivery time, and responsible party.

4. **Enhance AI Deployment Clarity**  
   - Replace placeholders with real context documents and well-defined autonomous vs. STOP conditions.

5. **Deepen Story Portal Relevance**  
   - Embed festival-community norms, moderation policies, and success metrics in the appendix.

Adopting Claude’s direction mitigates our greatest exposures while driving us toward a robust, actionable hybrid role framework for Story Portal.
